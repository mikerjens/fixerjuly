Production summary

This production explores the relationship between the Faroe Islands and the sea — fishing, food culture, identity, language, environmental pressure and the islands' political and cultural position. The shoot combines human stories, expert interviews, government perspective, Ólavsøka coverage and visual material from across the islands.

Production strategy

Main interviews are scheduled around Ólavsøka. 28–29 July are the Faroese national holiday days, used mainly for atmosphere, national identity, music, rowing, public events and B-roll.
The government interview is set for Monday 27 July, since 26 July is a Sunday.
Not permitted for filming: the Ólavsøka church service at Havnar Kirkja, and the Opening of the Parliamentary Year at Tinganes — flagged in red on the schedule.

Priority elements

Must have: Pál Weihe interview · Environmental / climate interview · Fishing family · Government interview · Ólavsøka & B-roll · Language and identity interview · Tunnel / undersea roundabout PTC · Strong Faroese visual material.

Should have: Kallsoy grandson on the boat (confirmed) · Boat access (confirmed) · Drone footage · Ólavsøka choir · Faroese chain dance if accessible · Vox pops · Harbour & coastal scenes.
